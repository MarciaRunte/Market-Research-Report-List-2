<p><ul><li>Air-cooled</li><li>Water-cooled</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The central chillers market is primarily divided into air-cooled and water-cooled types. Air-cooled chillers utilize ambient air for heat exchange, making them ideal for smaller facilities or where water supply is limited. They are generally easier to install and maintain. In contrast, water-cooled chillers employ water to dissipate heat, which is often more efficient for larger systems. While they require a cooling tower or a water source, they typically offer better energy efficiency and performance in substantial commercial applications.</p></p>
